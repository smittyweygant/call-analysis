# Interview Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to ensure consistent evaluation across candidates.

---

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Label | Definition |
|-------|-------|------------|
| 4 | Strong Hire | Exceeds requirements, would actively advocate for hire |
| 3 | Hire | Meets requirements, confident in success |
| 2 | Lean No | Some concerns, needs strong signal from other interviews |
| 1 | No Hire | Does not meet requirements or significant concerns |

---

## Interview Question Framework

### Technical Deep Dive
**Goal:** Assess depth of knowledge in core competency areas

Sample questions:
- "Walk me through a challenging technical problem you solved recently"
- "How would you approach [relevant technical scenario]?"
- "Explain [technical concept] as if I were new to this area"

**What to look for:**
- Structured thinking
- Depth vs breadth balance
- Ability to explain complex topics simply

### Behavioral Assessment
**Goal:** Understand how candidate operates in real situations

Sample questions:
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate"
- "Describe a project that didn't go as planned"
- "How do you prioritize when everything is urgent?"

**What to look for:**
- Self-awareness
- Learning from failures
- Collaboration style

### Role-Specific Scenarios
**Goal:** Evaluate fit for specific job requirements

Sample questions:
- "How would you handle [common role scenario]?"
- "What's your approach to [key job function]?"
- "Describe your ideal [work situation relevant to role]"

**What to look for:**
- Relevant experience
- Realistic expectations
- Growth potential

---

## Post-Interview Checklist

- [ ] Complete scorecard within 24 hours
- [ ] Provide specific examples for each rating
- [ ] Note any follow-up questions for next round
- [ ] Flag any concerns for hiring manager discussion
